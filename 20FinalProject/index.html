Retirement and Bliss

So you're finally an empty nester, and hopefully, a full pocket-er. So there it is. Right off the bat you know. I make up words. Empty nesters? Check. Bank account reasonably full? Check. Healthy and full of adventure? Check and check. Grab your checklist and let's check out some places we've visited, and our criteria for places we are considering retiring to.

Weather

No snow, sunny skies (for the most part), spring-like weather as a rule, not an exception.

Cost of Living!

Face it, we've all scrimped and saved (or come into it from generous relatives) to get this point. Costs of housing, health care, and travel fall into this category.

Outdoor Cafes

Everytime we travel, the cities/towns that delight us the most are the ones with outdoor cafes. Long afternoons sitting, drinking wine, and watching passergo-bys. A definite requirement of a retirement place.

Geocaching

So I have an addiction - besides sun, wine and travel. It's called geocaching. Using a hand-held GPS receiver, I seek out hidden treasures. Some call it a high-tech treasure hunt. Others - using multi-billion dollar satellite equipment to find tupperware in the woods.

Lisbon, Portugal

☆ ☆ ☆ ☆

Preferring the countryside to a cityview, I was surprised at how much I loved Lisbon! Visiting in July, we were hit with extreme temperatures of 80+degrees (F) a day, but the breezes through open windows and lack of biting insects made this a wonderful place.
